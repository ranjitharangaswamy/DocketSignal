Design thinking behind the theme dashboard

DocketSignal codes public Reddit legal-AI discourse into eight themes. Instead of repeating one excerpt card everywhere, each theme opens into a visualization that matches how practitioners actually talk about that concern.

Open the live showcase Jump to evidence panel

01 · The problem

One chart cannot carry eight different arguments

Frequency bars answer an important question: where does discourse concentrate? They do not answer what it feels like to read adoption pushback versus a vendor comparison thread versus a privilege-log win story. Those threads use different rhetorical registers — warnings, workshop complaints, policy memos, pipeline handoffs, role anxiety, product screenshots, emotion-laced review wins, and billing math.

Reusing a generic quote card for every theme would flatten that texture. It would also train viewers to skim past evidence because every panel looks the same. For a portfolio piece, I wanted the interface to demonstrate qualitative sensitivity: the visualization should respect the substance of each code, not only its count.

02 · Design insight

Match metaphor to discourse type, not just theme label

I treated each theme as a small design brief. Before picking a layout, I asked: if a researcher pinned this thread on a wall during synthesis, what artifact would they reach for? Accuracy threads read like citations to warn a colleague. Adoption friction belongs on a sticky-note culture map. Ethics posts resemble governance memos. Efficiency stories chain like workflow steps. Job-change anxiety attaches to roles, not abstract bars. Tool threads invite side-by-side vendor comparison. eDiscovery posts carry sentiment weight even when the topic is technical. Cost threads land naturally on a ledger.

Diversity in theme visualization is not decoration. It is a legibility strategy: when the form changes with the argument, readers slow down and connect excerpt to context.

03 · System choices

What stays consistent while the metaphors change

  • Warm legal palette Cream paper, oxblood accents, antique gold — scholarly and approachable, avoiding cold “legal tech SaaS blue” defaults.
  • One frequency layer for ranking The dot-and-bar chart handles comparison across themes; evidence panels handle depth within a theme. Separation keeps hierarchy clear.
  • Traceable excerpts Every layout preserves subreddit, score, and source link so visualization never trades away research provenance.
  • Static-first implementation Layout logic lives in theme-evidence.js with shared CSS tokens — no framework, portable for GitHub Pages portfolio hosting.
  • Responsive containment Sticky boards stack in narrow panels; comparison and feelings layouts collapse gracefully on small viewports.

04 · Process

From pipeline output to portfolio narrative

The analysis pipeline produces counts, excerpts, and memos. The showcase layer adds three reading modes: a scroll-driven story for the research question, an interactive frequency chart for prioritization, and the theme evidence panel for qualitative depth. Design thinking sits beside “Evidence” in the nav because that panel is where the visualization argument lives — it is the proof that the coding scheme respects discourse diversity.

If I extended this project, I would user-test whether distinct metaphors improve recall of theme definitions among legal researchers, and whether a “simple mode” toggle helps first-time viewers who prefer uniform cards. For portfolio purposes, the current approach optimizes for demonstrable craft: you can see the design decision in one click per theme.
